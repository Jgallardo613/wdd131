Aeroweather Website Plan (WDD 131 Project)

1. Site Name

Aeroweather

Description: The name 'Aeroweather' was chosen because it is a clear, concise, and professional name that immediately communicates the site's focus on providing current and forecast weather information.

2. Site Purpose

The purpose of Aeroweather is to provide a single, easy-to-use platform where users can track current weather conditions, 7-day forecasts, and other essential meteorological data for multiple cities around the world.

3. Scenarios

These questions represent the needs of our target audience (travelers and users monitoring distant family/locations).

  • Business Traveler Scenario: "What is the expected high and low temperature, and chance of precipitation, in three different cities (e.g., London, Tokyo, and Chicago) for my business trip next Tuesday?"
  • Family Check-in Scenario: "I need to quickly check the current weather conditions, including any severe weather alerts, for my family in Miami, Florida."

4. Color Schema

The selected colors are designed to be clean, professional, and associated with weather/sky elements.

  • Primary Color: Deep Sky Blue (#00BFFF)

    This vibrant blue will be used for primary elements such as main headings (h1, h2), interactive elements (links and buttons), and to represent positive or 'clear sky' weather conditions.

  • Secondary Color: White (#FFFFFF)

    White will serve as the main background color for the body of the site, providing a clean, bright, and modern feel, and ensuring high contrast for the text.

  • Text Color: Dark Gray (#333333)

    A dark gray will be used for all body text and paragraphs for a slightly softer, more professional look than pure black, ensuring great readability against the white background.

5. Typography

We will use a clean, modern, sans-serif font for readability across all devices.

  • Font: Arial / Helvetica (Sans-Serif)

    This font family will be used for all text (headings and body). Its high legibility and availability on most systems ensure a consistent and professional look for displaying numerical weather data and text descriptions.

6. Wireframe

The wireframes show the proposed layout of the home page for different screen sizes. (Note: These descriptions act as placeholders for the actual sketch images.)

Mobile View (Narrow Screen)

The mobile layout will prioritize a single column, making all elements easy to access. The layout will include:

  • Header (Menu button, Logo, City Search field)
  • Main Panel with Current Location Weather (Large icon, Temperature, Summary)
  • 3-Day Forecast Cards (Stacked vertically)
  • Scrollable List of Tracked Cities
  • Footer (Links, Copyright)

Large View (Wide Desktop)

The desktop layout uses a multi-panel approach to display more information simultaneously. The layout will include:

  • Header/Navigation (Full links, Logo, City Search)
  • Left Panel: Current Location Weather (Large detailed panel)
  • Right Panel: 7-Day Forecast (Cards displayed side-by-side)
  • Bottom Section: Quick-View Cards for Tracked Cities
  • Footer (Links, Copyright)
